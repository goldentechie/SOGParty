It's time to get serious about online Bitcoin wallets.

To use Carbon Wallet you need a really big password.

We have created a password for you. To use the CarbonWallet write your password down and keep it safe.

Make sure no-one is near you when you create this password. If anyone sees your password, or gets it from you, they can steal your Bitcoins.

We don't store your Bitcoins locally or on our servers.

Why should I use CarbonWallet ?

We are determined.

Actually we are a deterministic wallet (https://en.bitcoin.it/wiki/Deterministic_wallet). This means your bitcoin addresses are generated from your passphrase. Your passphrase is turned into a number which is then turned into Bitcoin keys. Your passphrase is also your backup. Simple.

We are a zero footprint wallet.

We don't store your Bitcoins locally or on our servers, only in your computer memory (RAM). No trace is left on your PC once you logout of your session. Thiefs have fewer angles of attack with CarbonWallet. Secure.

Carbon wallet can get cold.

Need to store large sums of Bitcoins ? CarbonWallet supports Cold Storage) https://en.bitcoin.it/wiki/Cold_storage, You can use CarbonWallet completely offline on a computer you know is clean and safe. (coming soon)

We will do everything we can to protect your money.

However you also need to take responsibility and follow these rules.

1. Never give out your passphrase.

There are several ways thiefs will try and get your passphrase. These attacks apply to all Bitcoin wallets so read up and be safe. a. Phishing (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phishing) Always make sure you are on the carbonwallet.com address and never click links in emails. b. Social engineering http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_engineering_(security) c. Keyloggers http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Keystroke_logging d. Malware. http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malware

2. Create three passphrases.

Think about passphrases the way you keep money in real life. a. A day to day passphrase. You could store a small amount of Bitocins here a bit like have a few notes in your wallet or purse. Write the passphrase down and carry it in your wallet. b. As a bank account. A passphrase that you only use in a safe environment, i.e. a computer with good virus protection that you are prety confident hasn't malware. Hold moderate amonts of Bitccins here. c. Your savings. A a passphrase you created as cold storage. Be very careful about bringing this passphrase online.

3. Never lose your passphrases.

Without the passhrases your coins are gone. Without the passhrase your coins are gone. Seriously, there is no way in this universe to retrieve CarbonWallet wallets without the passphrase. Here are several ways you can secure your passphrases. a. Write it down, keep it somewhere safe. This is probably the easist method, and the most secure. Write it, stick it in an envelope and hide it. b. Encrypt it, and store it on dropbox. c. Both a and b.

How does this work ? get all technical on me.

We are using 128bit passphrases.

According to "how big is your stack" The passphrases would take 5.00 hundred trillion trillion trillion trillion trillion trillion trillion trillion centuries under a massive cracking scenario. We generate a random passphrase from the following using the . Converts between 4-byte aligned strings and a human-readable sequence of words. Uses 1626 common words taken from wikipedia article: http://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Wiktionary:Frequency_lists/Contemporary_poetry Our passphrases are compatible with the Elektrum Bitcoin client.

We generate bitcoin addresses deterministically.

The passphrase is tunred into a 128bit key which is then stretched into 10 bitcoin addresses. There is no limit but for now 10 per passphrase should be adequate.
